Creating an apprenticeship vacancy as a training provider

You can create a vacancy in the ‘Recruitment’ section of your apprenticeship service account. If you need to create multiple or similar vacancies, you can clone them after you submit the vacancy.

We usually approve or reject vacancies within 24 hours.

Before you create a vacancy, learn about preventing discrimination in recruitment.

You will need the employer to give you permission to create apprenticeship vacancies on their behalf. They may choose to review the vacancies.

What you'll need

Before you start, you’ll need to know the:

  • vacancy name - this must relate to the apprenticeship training and use the word ‘apprentice’ or ‘apprenticeship’
  • training that the apprentice will take
  • training provider that will deliver the training
  • number of available positions
  • organisation’s name, address and location
  • start date, application closing date and whether the job is disability confident
  • duration of the apprenticeship and details of a typical working week
  • wage
  • places you want to get applications (on ‘find an apprenticeship’ or an external site)

You can save each vacancy as a draft and edit it before you submit it to us, as well as preview how it’ll look. If the employer has chosen to review the vacancy first, you may receive changes from them, you can find these in your recruitment dashboard.

We’ll give you a reference number for the vacancy after you submit it.

What happens next

You can check the status of each vacancy in your recruitment dashboard. The statuses are:

  • rejected
  • draft
  • live
  • closed
  • pending review

We’ll check the vacancy and let you know if you need to make any edits within 24 hours. You can view what edits you need to make on your ‘recruitment’ dashboard, but you can also choose to get email notifications.

Once we’ve approved the vacancy, you can view it on Find an Apprenticeship.

You can also increase the wage or extend the closing date after the vacancy has gone live.

Our new APIs make creating and displaying vacancies even easier

What are APIs?

APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) let systems share information and data with each other.

  • Recruitment API
    Our Recruitment API allows you to create vacancies on Find an apprenticeship using your own systems
  • Display adverts API
    Our Display adverts API allows you to display vacancies from Find an apprenticeship on your own websites

You will need someone with technical knowledge to connect your system with the APIs, for example a software developer.
